About me

Where research meets craft

I'm a UX/UI designer, and I'm doing my Master's in HCI at Newcastle. What I care about most is the moment a person sits in front of a screen, and whether it actually works for them.

Most of what I do starts with talking to people and working out what's getting in their way. From there I move into wireframes, prototypes, and testing until it feels right. I'd rather fix the real problem than add decoration on top of it.

Accessibility and digital wellbeing matter a lot to me. I like making things that respect people's time instead of fighting for their attention, and I keep an eye on how AI is changing the way we design. Whatever I build, I try to base it on what I've watched users actually do, not on what I assume they want.

How I work

Evidence in · clarity out
01

Research

I get to know the people I'm designing for — their situation, what they're trying to do, and where it tends to go wrong. Less guessing, more watching.

02

Define

Then I work out what the actual problem is, and what a good result would look like, before I open Figma.

03

Design

I sketch, wireframe, and build prototypes, testing ideas as I go. Accessibility goes in from the start, not bolted on at the end.

04

Validate

I put it in front of real people, see where they struggle, and fix what isn't working. Then I hand it over clean and documented.
